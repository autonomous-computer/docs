Choose this API for a financial-model refresh or research table that needs normalized statements and tag-level SEC facts with filing provenance. Raw SEC XBRL is the right source when your team wants to own all taxonomy parsing and reconciliation; a general financial-data tool may supply convenient values but may not expose the requested/resolved concept or the source trail your model review needs. The concrete job is to retrieve a normalized annual statement bundle and a tag-level fact series for one issuer, then decide whether their period and filing provenance support your model.

## Endpoints and boundaries

- `GET /v1/statements/all` returns a normalized balance sheet, income statement, and cash-flow bundle. It accepts issuer identity, annual or quarterly period selection, fiscal-year filters, and `limit` up to 40.
- `GET /v1/facts` returns normalized SEC company facts for a requested tag. The response identifies the requested and resolved tag.

These routes normalize SEC XBRL; they do not eliminate issuer-specific concepts, extension tags, period differences, amendments, or restatements. Do not compare annual and quarterly values as though they were interchangeable, and do not treat a missing tag as proof a metric was not disclosed.

## Selection test

This is a fit when the model needs both a reusable normalized layer and the evidence to resolve exceptions. It is not a replacement for an issuer-specific accounting policy or a restatement decision. The proof passes when the team can reconcile at least one statement line to the selected fact series after checking form, period, unit, resolved tag, and filing provenance.

## Proof of concept

```bash
# Retrieve one normalized annual statement bundle.
curl --fail-with-body -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/statements/all?ticker=MSFT&period=annual&limit=1"

# Retrieve up to four annual observations for a requested revenue concept.
curl --fail-with-body -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/facts?ticker=MSFT&tag=Revenues&form=10-K&limit=4"
```

Compare the reported statement line to the fact series only after aligning `form`, fiscal period, `periodEnd`, `filedAt`, and unit. Inspect `resolvedTag` before assuming the requested concept was used.

## Expected evidence

For every model input, retain requested/resolved tag, value, unit, form, fiscal labels, period end, filing date, accession number and filing URL from per-point provenance, plus the enclosing `requestId` and `traceparent`. Values are returned in raw units; apply display formatting or unit conversion only in a clearly defined model layer.

## Failure modes

- `401` or `403`: check the server-side key and route entitlement.
- Empty fact data: confirm issuer and tag, then inspect `resolvedTag`; issuers can use another valid concept or taxonomy.
- Apparent reconciliation failure: recheck units, annual versus quarterly periods, the filing date, and whether an amendment or restatement is part of the intended model policy.
- High-volume refreshes: cache historical observations, bound concurrency, and quote the actual cadence using [Pricing and limits](/evaluate/pricing-and-limits).
