---

Evaluate the retrieval layer before generation. A passing test returns a passage a reader can locate in an SEC filing and makes the gap between source text and interpretation explicit.

## Minimal loop

```bash
curl -sS -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/search/semantic?q=supply%20chain%20risk&ticker=TSLA&form=10-K&mode=hybrid&limit=5"

curl -sS -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/filings/ACCESSION/sections/item_1a?ticker=TSLA&form=10-K"
```

Replace `ACCESSION` with the search result's `accession`. Save `source_url`, `section_key`, and any available character range with the text you provide to a model.

## Evidence boundary

Keep these distinct in your answer:

1. Retrieved filing text.
2. Filing identity: accession number, form, filing date, and SEC URL.
3. Your application's interpretation.

Do not turn a missing match into a claim about what an issuer did not disclose. A degraded citation is a reason to open the filing, not permission to invent a span.

## Production checklist

- Use semantic `mode` explicitly after evaluation.
- Fetch the cited section before quoting it.
- Preserve `requestId`, `traceparent`, provenance, and freshness where supplied.
- Treat a `503` vector-search failure as unavailable retrieval, not an empty result set.
