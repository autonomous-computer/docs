## What to check

- Starter grant state, remaining calls, and whether PAYG has been activated.
- The meter class and meter family for each endpoint in the workflow.
- The rights boundary: evaluation, internal use, customer-facing embedding, redistribution, resale, or Bulk Factor Export.
- Budget soft caps, approval thresholds, hard caps, prepaid-credit state, and current invoice-period usage.
- AI-query quota fields where the route uses that quota family.
- Rate-limit, retry, metering, billing, and trace headers that your client preserves.
- Whether a billing change requires a WorkOS browser session instead of an API key.

## Evaluation path

<Steps>
  <Step title="Map the workflow to endpoint families">
    Filing search, section extraction, facts, ownership, delivery events, email notifications, intelligence, market data, semantic search, MCP, and artifact routes can have different meter classes, unit prices, and rate limits.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check live billing state">
    Read `GET /v1/billing`, `GET /v1/billing/rates`, and `GET /v1/limits` before high-fanout work. Do not infer plan posture from a cached note, docs page, or UI screenshot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quote repeated work">
    Use `POST /v1/billing/quote` with a `meterClass`, or a `method` and `path`, before repeating section extraction, compare workflows, artifact generation, intelligence queries, semantic search, or market-data reads.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Respect auth and billing permissions">
    Use `x-api-key` for machine traffic. Billing-control actions require a WorkOS browser-session bearer token with `billing:manage`, `admin:operator`, or `*`; API keys are rejected for those mutations. Paid billing and payment setup also require current legal acceptance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the right rights boundary">
    Starter grant is for evaluation. PAYG, Personal, and Team are for internal use. Redistribution, embedding in customer-facing products, white-label, resale, and Bulk Factor Export require Commercial.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Billing and limit signals

Preserve these fields in logs, agent notes, and support requests:

- `requestId` and `traceparent`
- `SECAPI-Meter-Class`
- `SECAPI-Plan-Key`
- `SECAPI-Billing-State` and `SECAPI-Billing-Status`
- `SECAPI-Free-Grant-Remaining`
- `SECAPI-Credit-Balance-Cents` when present
- `SECAPI-Token-Count`, `SECAPI-Token-Count-Estimated`, and `SECAPI-Estimated-Cost`
- `SECAPI-AI-Quota-Limit`, `SECAPI-AI-Quota-Used`, `SECAPI-AI-Quota-Remaining`, and `SECAPI-AI-Quota-Reset` when present

Stop instead of retrying when the API returns `payg_pending_card`, `insufficient_credits`, `billing_budget_approval_required`, `billing_budget_hard_cap_exceeded`, or `restricted_read_only`.

## Plan and payment boundaries

- New organizations start with a one-time `150` call `sandbox_grant` allowance.
- Pay As You Go activation creates a card-backed, prepaid-credit wallet; after activation, usage debits that wallet.
- Manual top-ups are available only for PAYG organizations that already debit credits.
- Personal, Team, and Commercial usage is billed through the plan and budget system, not the PAYG wallet.
- Personal includes one seat and three API keys; Team includes five seats and ten API keys; Commercial includes five seats and twenty-five API keys.
- Commercial has a published contract floor of `$18,000/yr`; final scope, rights, and billing terms are set in the commercial agreement.
- Payment setup, checkout, PAYG enablement, credit top-ups, auto-top-up, refunds, saved payment methods, billing portal sessions, starter-grant reset, and budget changes require `billing:manage`, `admin:operator`, or `*`.

Paid tiers do not use a fixed monthly AI-query count by default. Sandbox usage has a trial AI-query limit; paid usage is governed by metered pricing, rate limits, payment state, and budget gates.
