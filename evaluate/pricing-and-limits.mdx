Treat commercial and operational limits as part of the proof of concept. The job here is to map one proposed workflow to its API calls, inspect the authenticated organization's current posture, and obtain a quote before broad fanout.

## Live preflight endpoints

- `GET /v1/billing` returns the organization's billing snapshot, including pricing posture and budget controls.
- `GET /v1/billing/rates` returns the public catalog, starter-grant metadata, plan metadata, and meter-family rates.
- `GET /v1/limits` returns the current account limits.
- `POST /v1/billing/quote` quotes a billable workflow or meter class against the current plan and budget gates.

Use `x-api-key` for these machine reads. Billing mutations are browser-session actions and require the documented billing permissions; do not use a machine key for checkout, payment, credit, or budget changes.

## Proof of concept

```bash
curl --fail-with-body -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \

# Send a documented quote body for the workflow or route you intend to repeat.
curl --fail-with-body -X POST -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \

```

Use the [billing quote reference](/api-reference/platform/post-v1-billing-quote) if the workflow needs a different request shape. Do not estimate a fleet-wide cost from a pricing screenshot or a one-off response.

## Verified plan and rights boundaries

The authored plan catalog currently describes a one-time 150-call starter grant, PAYG as the default paid path, Personal and Team self-serve plans, and Commercial as the plan family for redistribution and external commercial rights. Starter-grant use is for evaluation; PAYG, Personal, and Team are for in-house use. Customer-facing embedding, redistribution, white-labeling, resale, and Bulk Factor Export require Commercial.

Plan pricing, meter rates, limits, and budget state can change, so the account's live endpoints above are the source for a go/no-go decision. See [Plans and pricing](/plans-and-pricing) for the current published catalog and [Enterprise and commercial posture](/enterprise-commercial) for rights details.

## Expected evidence

Record the effective plan and billing state, endpoint or meter class, rate/quote response, budget and limit signals, anticipated cadence, and `requestId`. Preserve response headers including `SECAPI-Meter-Class`, `SECAPI-Plan-Key`, `SECAPI-Billing-State`, `SECAPI-Billing-Status`, and `Retry-After` when supplied.

## Failure modes

- `401`: the machine credential did not reach the API or is not accepted.
- `402`: billing, credits, plan access, or a budget policy blocks the request; resolve the state before retrying.
- `429`: honor `Retry-After` and lower fanout rather than immediately retrying.
- A workflow crosses an external-use boundary: pause implementation and obtain commercial terms before exposing SEC API-derived output to customers or public users.
