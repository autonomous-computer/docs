description: "Plan filing and derived-data workflows around documented history and route-specific availability"
---

Start by retrieving the source filing for the issuer, form, and date range your workflow needs. Then request the particular section, fact, statement, or derived surface; filing discovery does not guarantee that a derived representation is available.

```bash
curl -sS \
  -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/filings?ticker=AAPL&form=10-K&limit=25"
```

## Public history boundary

SEC API documents a nine-year searchable floor for filing and section workflows. Release-blocking completeness checks cover the two most recent completed years for required filing forms. The current year has an availability floor, not a completed-year completeness guarantee.

Older records can be available, but their density and derived representations vary by issuer, form, and filing format. An inventory target is not an extension of the public history boundary.

| Need | First request | Confirm before use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Identify a source filing | [Filings](/api-reference/filings/get-v1-filings) or [latest filing](/api-reference/filings/get-v1-filings-latest) | issuer, form, filing date, accession number, and source URL |
| Read filing text or a section | the applicable render or section route | that the filing and requested section return content |
| Use a normalized fact or statement | the applicable facts or statements route | period, units, provenance, and disclosed field availability |
| Build an event or ownership workflow | the relevant event or ownership route | date range, form family, and response capability or warnings |

## Do not equate related surfaces

- A filing manifest does not mean every section or derived output is ready.
- A source document does not mean every fact is normalized or comparable across issuers.
- A result for one issuer, form, or year does not establish coverage for another.
- A successful response does not establish that it is current enough for your decision.

For market calendars, use returned `coverage`, `confidence`, and `statusNote`. `XNYS` and `XNAS` have configured holiday coverage; other documented markets can report weekend-only or tentative coverage. See [Methodology registry](/methodology-registry).

## Make partial availability explicit

Preserve the source accession number and URL, then store any returned period, provenance, capability, warning, or degradation fields. Return unavailable or partial results to your caller explicitly instead of substituting unrelated data. Retain `Request-Id` for unexpected results.

SEC API does not publish an equal-density guarantee beyond the stated history floor, a universal real-time lag, or a promise to normalize every EDGAR exhibit and filing family. Read [Freshness and source evidence](/freshness-and-trust) before applying an output to a time-sensitive workflow.
