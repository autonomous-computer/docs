---
title: "Special situations"
description: "Track SEC filing-derived M&A, tender offers, going-private, spin-off, activist, restructuring, and bankruptcy situations by lifecycle and timeline."
---

Use special situations to follow a filing-derived situation across its lifecycle, associated filings, events, dates, and available deal terms rather than treating each disclosure as an isolated record.

## What you can do

- List situations by type, subtype, status, ticker, sector, market-cap bucket, country, announcement date, or update time.
- Filter with `forms` to expand supported EDGAR forms to the situation types they open, or query one form directly.
- Read a reverse-chronological situation event feed, upcoming calendar dates, aggregate stats, or closed-situation cohorts.
- Retrieve one situation, its paginated filing timeline, or its compact summary.
- Request `enrich=false` for a minimal projection when scanning a wide result set.

## Endpoints

| Method | Path | What it returns |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations`](/api-reference/situations) | A filtered, paginated situation list. |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations/feed`](/api-reference/situations) | A reverse-chronological event feed. |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations/calendar`](/api-reference/situations) | Upcoming situation dates. |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations/stats`](/api-reference/situations/get-v1-situations-stats) | Counts by type, status, sector, and market-cap bucket. |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations/performance`](/api-reference/situations/get-v1-situations-performance) | Closed-situation outcome cohorts. |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations/by-form/{form}`](/api-reference/situations) | Situations associated with an EDGAR form. |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations/{situation_id}`](/api-reference/situations/get-v1-situations-situation-id) | One situation with its filing timeline. |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations/{situation_id}/filings`](/api-reference/situations) | The paginated filing timeline for one situation. |
| `GET` | [`/v1/situations/{situation_id}/summary`](/api-reference/situations) | A compact situation summary. |

## Example

```bash
curl -sS \
  -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/situations?types=merger&statuses=pending&limit=25"
```

Follow a returned situation ID with its timeline endpoint, retain the filing identities and source references from the events, and use the original filing when a fact will support a decision.

## Boundaries to keep in view

- The authenticated `/v1/situations` API is separate from public embed routes. Use an organization API key for product requests.
- Situations are filing-derived. They do not confirm that a transaction will close or that a disclosed date, term, or status remains current.
- `enrich=false` changes the returned projection, not the billed request class. It is useful for payload size, not quota savings.
- `limit` is 1 through 100. Keep filters unchanged while advancing the returned cursor; a missing situation or timeline is not proof that no related event exists.
