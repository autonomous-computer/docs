---
title: "Institutional ownership and insider trading API"
description: "Build source-cited ownership research with 13F, Form 3/4/5, and Schedule 13D/13G filing records"
---

SEC API gives an application or research workflow structured records for three different ownership questions: a manager's reported 13F holdings, an insider's reported transaction, and a beneficial owner's 13D or 13G disclosure. Each record is filing-derived, so your output can retain the accession number and source URL needed to inspect the original disclosure.

## Choose the right disclosure

| Question | Route | What the result establishes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What did this manager report at a quarter end? | `GET /v1/owners/13f` | A selected, normalized 13F report |
| What changed between a manager's latest reports? | `POST /v1/owners/13f/compare` | A comparison of the latest two parsable reports |
| Which managers reported this issuer? | `GET /v1/owners/institutional/ticker` | A holder ranking for a reported 13F cohort |
| What insider transactions were reported? | `GET /v1/insiders` | Normalized records from Forms 3, 4, and 5 |
| Which beneficial-ownership reports were filed? | `GET /v1/owners/13d-13g` | 13D/13G-family disclosure records |

## First useful result

Retrieve recent Form 4 records for an issuer:

```bash
curl -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/insiders?ticker=AAPL&forms=4&limit=10"
```

Retrieve a manager's latest reported 13F holdings:

```bash
curl -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/owners/13f?cik=0001067983&limit=20"
```

Both results are disclosure records, not conclusions. Store `accessionNumber`, the filing URL in `provenance`, relevant dates, and `requestId` before grouping, scoring, or alerting on the data.

## Build a defensible ownership record

1. Query a narrow issuer, manager, or owner scope first.
2. Retain the source fields with the normalized record.
3. Use the report date and filing date as separate values.
4. Read the cited filing when a footnote, amendment, security class, or transaction term could change the conclusion.

For example, get a beneficial-ownership queue for one issuer:

```bash
curl -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/owners/13d-13g?ticker=AAPL&forms=SC%2013D,SC%2013G&limit=25"
```

Use `forms` only for the SEC form families you intend to evaluate. A later amendment may supersede important facts from an earlier filing, so preserve the filing form and amendment status in the research record.

## Limits to keep in the product

- A 13F is delayed and limited to its reporting scope. It is not a manager's live, complete portfolio.
- A Form 4 code does not, by itself, establish motive or open-market activity. Read the filing and its footnotes when that distinction matters.
- A 13D or 13G is a dated beneficial-ownership disclosure, not a fully diluted cap table or an exhaustive view of a holder's exposure.
- The holder route reflects its returned reporting cohort. Do not combine it with another period without labeling the dates.
