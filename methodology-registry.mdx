Use this page to evaluate a derived result before relying on it. SEC API returns source-backed records and derived views, but a response does not establish universal coverage, real-time delivery, or cross-issuer comparability.

```bash
curl -sS -H "x-api-key: $SECAPI_API_KEY" \
  "https://api.secapi.ai/v1/market/calendar?market=XNYS"
```

For every material result, retain the endpoint, parameters, period, returned source fields, and any capability, warning, degradation, or status-note fields. The endpoint reference is authoritative for accepted parameters and response shape.

## Shared terms

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Source-backed | Tied to a disclosed filing, an official public source, or both. |
| Derived | SEC API normalizes, classifies, compares, or calculates from source material. |
| `capability` | A route-specific result state such as `supported`, `not_disclosed`, `degraded`, or `unavailable`; not a platform-wide quality grade. |
| `statusNote` | A nullable explanation where a route returns a limited, partial, or unavailable result. |

## Market schedules and index data

`GET /v1/market/calendar` returns a session status for a requested market and date. Read `coverage`, `confidence`, and `statusNote` with `sessionStatus`. `configured_holidays` means the market has configured holiday coverage; `weekend_only` does not establish local-holiday coverage. `tentative` means the route cannot confirm the market-day posture from configured coverage. `XNYS` and `XNAS` have configured holiday coverage.

`GET /v1/market/indices` and `GET /v1/market/indices/constituents` expose inventory and public constituent surfaces subject to their returned rights posture. `inventory_only` does not make a constituent roster public. A `null` weight or `unpublished` weight method does not permit an inferred weight, and `positionRank` is roster order rather than a portfolio weight. See [market calendar](/api-reference/market/get-v1-market-calendar), [indices](/api-reference/market/get-v1-market-indices), and [index constituents](/api-reference/market/get-v1-market-indices-constituents).

## Filings, facts, statements, and traces

Filing discovery, rendering, section extraction, and traces begin with SEC filing records. Use accession number and filing URL to anchor a result. A filing response does not establish that each section, page anchor, or trace region exists.

Facts and statement routes normalize issuer disclosures. Check source, period, unit, and `periodBasis` where returned before comparing values. Segment labels and hierarchies are issuer disclosures, not a universal taxonomy. For company financial balance-sheet records, `commonEquity` uses disclosed common-stockholders' equity where available and can use stockholders' equity less disclosed preferred stock; evaluate returned period and source context before comparison.

Trace routes can return filing-backed or source-record evidence. A page-only trace is page-level evidence, not proof of an exact resolved source span. See [filings](/api-reference/filings/get-v1-filings), [sections](/api-reference/filings/get-v1-filings-latest-sections-section-key), [facts](/api-reference/facts/get-v1-facts), [statements](/api-reference/statements/get-v1-statements), and [traces](/api-reference/traces/get-v1-traces).

## Filing-derived event and analysis routes

M&A events are inferred from public-company filings and relevant exhibits; they are not a complete deal database. Their status should be read with filing date and accession number, not as a live transaction-status feed.

Risk categories are deterministic coverage for the latest covered Item 1A section, not a complete issuer risk assessment. Their confidence is not a probability of loss or outcome; use returned signals and snippets as the supporting filing evidence.

The volatility score is a derived SEC-disclosure signal, not market data, a forecast, or investment advice. Interpret it with its factor breakdown, methodology version, confidence, and `degradedReasons`. When returned freshness is stale or degraded, use the returned reason before relying on the score.

Earnings materials are limited to SEC-furnished material reachable through the relevant filing path. Their `coverage` describes the usable material found for that filing, not transcript completeness outside SEC-furnished content. Enforcement actions search official SEC litigation releases, administrative proceedings, and AAERs; use the linked source document for legal or factual conclusions.

See [M&A events](/api-reference/events/get-v1-events-ma), [risk categories](/api-reference/filings/get-v1-filings-latest-risk-categories), [volatility score](/api-reference/signals/get-v1-signals-volatility), [earnings materials](/api-reference/earnings/get-v1-earnings-transcripts), and [enforcement actions](/api-reference/events/get-v1-events-enforcement).

## Use derived results responsibly

Retrieve the source-backed record where the route provides one. Check the documented source, period, unit, and state fields. Preserve source identifiers and request context. Make unknown, partial, degraded, and unavailable results visible in your application rather than turning a single result into a claim about corpus completeness or currentness.
